About

iPhemap is comprehensive regularly updated database that aims to provide a field synopsis and catalog all of the in vitro neuronal disease phenotypes from induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) derived from patients with neurological diseases.

You can search cellular and molecular phenotypes from 62 published reports. We characterized 428 distinct cellular phenotypes and the resulting relationships between gene and phenotype into phenogenetic map that can be used to build new hypothese sin the field of neurological disease modeling, and to identify potential new opportunities to design novel drug strategies.

iPheMap comprises a comprehensive catalog of phenogenetic profiles from patient derived-iPSCs from highly curated, published reports and returns:

  1. Cellular phenotypes reporting morphological, functional, and process changes from iPSCs, NSCs, oligodendrocytes, astrocytes, and neurons with genetic mutations linked to neurological diseases.
  2. Molecular phenotypes, which stem from the microarray analysis, are derived from the functional annotations associated with previously established genome ontology. These molecular phenotypes are presented as treemaps, sized according to their degree of statistical significance.
  3. Dysregulated pathways also arising from genome ontology, which reflect disrupted canonical pathways.

For details on the background and system biology methods used in iPhemap, see the Methods section. The website is developed and maintained by the Imitola Stem Cell Group.
