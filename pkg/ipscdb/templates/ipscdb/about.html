About

The iPS cell phenogenetic map project “iPhemap” is comprehensive database that aims to provide a field synopsis and catalog all of the in vitro neuronal disease phenotypes from induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) derived from patients with neurological diseases. You can search cellular and molecular phenotypes from 79 published reports. We characterized 550 distinct cellular phenotypes and the resulting relationships between gene and phenotype into a phenogenetic map that can be used to build new hypotheses in the field of neurological disease modeling, and to identify potential new opportunities to design novel drug strategies. The project comprises a comprehensive catalog of phenogenetic profiles from patient derived-iPSCs from highly curated, published reports and returns: 1) Cellular phenotypes from iPSCs, neural stem cells, oligodendrocytes, astrocytes, and neurons with genetic mutations linked linked to neurological diseases. 2) Molecular phenotypes and dysregulated pathways, when available, from gene ontology analyses of gene expression profiles.
