About

iPhemap is a comprehensive, regularly updated database that aims to provide a field synopsis and catalog all of the in vitro neuronal disease phenotypes from induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) derived from patients with neurological diseases.

You can search cellular and molecular phenotypes from 79 published reports. We characterized 550 distinct cellular phenotypes and the resulting relationships between gene and phenotype into a phenogenetic map that can be used to build new hypotheses in the field of neurological disease modeling, and to identify potential new opportunities to design novel drug strategies.

iPhemap comprises a comprehensive catalog of phenogenetic profiles from patient derived-iPSCs from highly curated, published reports and returns:

  1. Cellular phenotypes reporting morphological, functional, and process changes from iPSCs, NSCs, oligodendrocytes, astrocytes, and neurons with genetic mutations linked to neurological diseases.
  2. Molecular phenotypes, derived from microarray analysis and presented as treemaps, describing the associated gene ontology terms.
  3. Dysregulated pathways highlighting disrupted pathways from microarray analysis.

For details on the background and system biology methods used in iPhemap, see the Methods section. The website is developed and maintained by the Imitola Laboratory.

The Imitola Laboratory at The Ohio State University Neurological Institute has developed this resource for identifying phenotype-genotype relationship and cataloging curated phenotypes in patient-derived models of neurological disease.
